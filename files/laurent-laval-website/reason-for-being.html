The Origin

For 40 years, I have studied leadership and decision making as an international role holder, and as a trusted advisor and coach to hundreds of leaders and executive teams.

Most leadership methods focus on externals such as strategy, performance, risk, and communication. Almost none begin with the first level of leadership: knowing who we truly are. This missing link led me to develop the Reason for Being methodology.

What is a Reason for Being?

"Who am I, when I make a decision?"

I help people and teams align their decisions, contribution, and leadership style with their true purpose. A Reason for Being is expressed in a single sentence that captures:

  • our natural talent
  • our unique contribution
  • our personal way of acting

It is our identity, a small flame, lit at birth, often hidden yet stable. Once uncovered, it lights our professional and personal paths.

Why It Matters

Leaders facing the same situation may make very different decisions. But when one leader makes inconsistent choices over time, the team loses coherence.

A Reason for Being guides purposeful decisions. It clarifies what to consider, accept, or reject. It shows when to change course or stop. It strengthens resilience. Without this clarity, decisions become fragmented and exhausting.

Collective Reason for Being

A collective Reason for Being highlights the strengths, talents, contributions, and style of a team or organization. It harmonizes with the company's mission and vision. It is especially valuable during:

  • new management
  • mergers or acquisitions
  • reorganizations
  • weak inter-departmental communication

Organizational Impact

A Reason for Being strengthens organizational clarity and performance. When people understand their purpose and contribution, collaboration becomes more fluid, and teams act with greater coherence.

Guided by individual and collective purpose:

  • Leaders translate strategy into clear projects and priorities.
  • Energy and engagement rise through renewed coherence.
  • Talent strategies become more accurate and meaningful.
  • Trust, agility and innovation grow naturally.

People give their best when they feel aligned with who they are, when their work has meaning, and their distinctive value is truly seen.
